## What We've Learned

After 18 years and a dual CKD + hyperthyroid diagnosis, here's what's actually worked:

**Stability is the win.** Alice held 8.52 lbs from November 2024 through May 2026. For a cat who lost 7 lbs over 4 years, stable weight is a genuine victory worth tracking.

**The vet visit is the unlock.** You can do a lot at home — sub-Q fluids, supplements, monitoring — but you need the lab numbers first. The bloodwork tells you what you're actually dealing with.

**Don't force the supplements.** Dr. Boone's guidance: appetite is the top priority. An Azodyl capsule that makes her refuse food does more harm than good. The food comes first.

**Daily logs catch things you'd otherwise miss.** A single day of "ate less" is nothing. Three days in a row is a pattern worth calling the vet about.
