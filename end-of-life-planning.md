# End-of-Life Planning — A Compassionate Guide

This is one of the hardest documents to write, but having a plan removes some of the weight when the time comes. Fill this out when you're calm and not in crisis — so you don't have to figure it out in the worst moment.

---

## Quality of Life Threshold

Before making any end-of-life decisions, use the QoL score (see [quality-of-life.md](quality-of-life.md)).

- **Below 35/70 consistently** — talk to your vet about what's happening and what the path forward looks like
- This is a conversation, not an automatic decision — your vet is your partner

---

## Types of End-of-Life Care

**Hospice/comfort care:** Focus shifts from treatment to comfort. Keep pain managed, keep them eating and warm, minimize stress. Quality over quantity.

**In-home euthanasia:** A vet comes to your home. Your pet passes in their own space, surrounded by family, without the stress of a carrier and a clinic. This is an option worth knowing about in advance.

**Clinic euthanasia:** Performed at a vet's office. Sometimes necessary depending on circumstances.

## Things to Prepare

- [ ] Decide on burial vs. cremation (individual vs. communal) — research local options and costs
- [ ] Pick a resting place or decide what to do with ashes
- [ ] Note who should be present / notified
- [ ] Have a small amount of money set aside or a plan for the cost
- [ ] Optional: create a keepsake (paw print clay, photo album, letter)

---

## A Note on Timing

There's no perfect moment. The goal is to prevent suffering, not to hold on as long as biologically possible. When more days are bad than good — when the things they love (food, warmth, connection) no longer bring them comfort — that's the signal.

You will second-guess yourself. That's okay. Talk to your vet. Talk to someone you trust.

You've given them a good life. That will always be true.

---

## After They're Gone

- [ ] Let yourself grieve — this is real loss
- [ ] Tell people who knew them
- [ ] Take time before making any decisions about future pets
- [ ] Consider a small memorial — it helps
- [ ] Update or archive the care tracker as a record of their life
