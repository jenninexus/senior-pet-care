# Red Flag Reference Card — Senior Cat

**Print this and put it on the fridge.**

---

## Call Vet NOW (same day)

- Not eating for more than 24 hours
- Vomiting more than 2x in one day
- Straining to urinate or no urination in 12+ hours
- Skin turgor slow (pinch back of neck — doesn't snap back quickly)
- Sudden weight loss (weigh weekly)
- Excessive lethargy — won't get up, won't respond normally
- Drinking dramatically more or less water than usual

## Go to Emergency Vet (right now)

- Open-mouth breathing or panting (cats do NOT pant normally)
- Pale, white, grey, or blue gums
- Seizures or collapse
- Difficulty walking or sudden loss of coordination
- Crying out in pain
- Heart rate over 220 BPM at rest

---

## Hydration Check (Skin Turgor Test)

1. Gently pinch the skin on the back of the neck
2. Release
3. Snaps back immediately = hydrated
4. Slow to return (1+ seconds) = possible dehydration → call vet
5. Stays tented = dehydration → emergency

---

## Heart Rate Check

- Normal resting: 140–220 BPM
- Place hand on left side of chest just behind front leg
- Count beats for 15 seconds, multiply by 4

## Common Senior Cat Symptoms & What They Mean

| Symptom | Possible cause | Action |
|---------|---------------|--------|
| Drinking a lot more water | CKD, diabetes, hyperthyroid | Call vet — needs bloodwork |
| Weight loss despite eating | Hyperthyroidism, CKD, cancer | Call vet |
| Vomiting | Nausea (CKD), hairballs, infection | Track frequency; >2x/day call vet |
| Hiding more than usual | Pain, nausea, stress | Monitor closely; call vet if >24hrs |
| Litter box changes | UTI, CKD, constipation | Call vet if straining or blood |
| Coat looks dull/matted | B12 deficiency, pain, can't groom | Mention at next vet visit |
| Wobbly or unsteady gait | Neurological, low potassium, pain | Call vet same day |
